The worldmap is based on data obtained from the Topojson library, the colours themselves are meaningless, they were chosen so as to not have two similarly coloured countries adjacent to one another. That being said when you click on any location on the worldmap it'll update the adjacent barchart to give you an indication of some of the basic neccesities available in that country. (other functionalities include moving and zooming)
The data about basic life neccesities in countries were obtained from the the world bank's databank the datasets chosen were based on a personal opinion as to what are decent basic indicators for the quality of life in a given country, urban vs rural. The default setting is the global average, indicated as "default"

The Story

Across the globe people have varying degrees of access to the basic neccesities to live a relatively comfortable life. These are: access to electricity, drinkable water and improved sanitation. It is part my belief that the lack of these basic neccessities form part of the reason why theres a large influx of people coming from areas like sub-Saharan Africa and countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan, to the Western World and more specifically Europe.

For instance when one goes to compare Western Europe to rural Africa for example, the differences are simply astonishing. The luxaries and neccessities we take for granted here in the Europe and most of the globe, are largly absent in rural Africa or even urban Africe.

Compare for instance France to Mali. France has a tremendously high GDP compared to Mali, which is its own is already quite the economic incentive for people to want to emmigrate from Mali to Europe. But more importantly the difference in basic neccesities to life a relatively comfortable life is simply astonishing. In France more than 95% of the population (urban and rural) has access to electricity, improved sanitation and improved water sources.Compare that to Mali where only 77% of the population has access to an improved water source (64% of the rural population, 97% of the urban population), or 27% of the population has access to electricity (51% urban vs 12% rural).

This is a story that repeats itself all across Africa and many other parts of the globe.

Take war and general instability out of the equation, is it any wonder why so many people would want to emigrate to the western world? where the standards of living are higher and everyone has access to ample clean, drinkable water, electricty and basic santitation.
